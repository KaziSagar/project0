Kazi Nazrul Islam (Bengali: কাজী নজরুল ইসলাম, Bengali pronunciation: [kāzi nôzrul îslām] (About this soundlisten), 24 May 1899 – 29 August 1976) was a Bengali Bangladeshi poet, writer, musician and the national poet of Bangladesh.[2][3] Popularly known as Nazrul, he produced a large body of poetry and music with themes that included religious devotion and rebellion against oppression.[4] Nazrul's activism for political and social justice earned him the title of "Rebel Poet".[5] His compositions form the avant-garde genre of Nazrul Geeti (Music of Nazrul).[6][7][8][9] Born in a Bengali Muslim Kazi family, Nazrul Islam received religious education and as a young man worked as a muezzin at a local mosque. He learned about poetry, drama, and literature while working with the rural theatrical group Letor Dal. He joined the British Indian Army in 1917.
